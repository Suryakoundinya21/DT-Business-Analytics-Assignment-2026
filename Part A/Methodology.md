# Part A: Methodology Document
### Target Company Research — Bengaluru, Precision Auto Components & Aerospace Engineering
#### DeepThought Business Analytics Internship Assignment

---

## City Selection: Bengaluru

**Rationale:** Bengaluru is India's aerospace and defence electronics capital. It hosts:
- HAL, ISRO, DRDO, NAL, GTRE — all generating a dense private supplier ecosystem
- KIADB Aerospace Park (Devanahalli/Bagalur) — India's dedicated aerospace SEZ
- Peenya Industrial Area — one of India's largest industrial clusters, concentration of precision engineering MSMEs
- Bommasandra-Jigani corridor — growing precision machining cluster

The city produces the strongest density of aerospace-grade precision manufacturers in India, making it the highest-yield city for this segment.

---

## Segment Selection: Precision Auto Components & Aerospace Engineering (Basket C)

**Rationale:** Bengaluru's Basket C precision engineering segment sits at the intersection of:
- A 50-year legacy of automotive component manufacturing (Peenya)
- A growing aerospace machining ecosystem (KIADB Aerospace Park, HAL supply chain)
- Strong China+1 and Make-in-India tailwinds

This makes it both a rich sourcing pool and a strategically aligned segment for DeepThought's Federer ICP.

---

## Research Process

### Step 1: Anchor companies identified first

Started with the best-known players in Bengaluru aerospace to calibrate what the ICP looks like in this city:
- **Sansera Engineering** — known listed company in precision forging. Revenue check: Rs.3,498Cr — immediately disqualified.
- **Dynamatic Technologies** — known defence/aerospace player. Revenue: Rs.1,621Cr — disqualified.
- **BEL / HAL** — obvious PSUs. Auto-disqualified.

**Key learning from Step 1:** Bengaluru's aerospace precision engineering segment is dominated by companies that have *already scaled past Rs.500Cr*. The ICP-sized companies exist but are harder to find and less visible. This set the expectation: I needed to dig deeper than the obvious names.

### Step 2: Search for mid-market companies in the Rs.50–500Cr band

Searched:
- "Bengaluru precision auto components aerospace manufacturer BSE SME IATF AS9100"
- "Bengaluru aerospace CNC manufacturer promoter-driven 100cr 300cr"
- BSE SME / NSE Emerge IPO filings from Bengaluru in aerospace/engineering

**Key finds:**
- **JJG Aero** — surfaced from a January 2026 press release about $30M Series B from Norwest. Verified: KIADB Bommasandra, founded 2008, 35% CAGR, Collins Aerospace / Safran / GE Aerospace customers, NADCAP 30+ special processes. Strongest ICP match.
- **Datasol (Bangalore) Ltd** — surfaced from BSE SME DRHP filing (Sep 2025). Revenue doubled 2x in 2 years, AS9100D certified, defence/space embedded electronics focus.
- **Centum Electronics** — surfaced in manufacturing company lists. Verified: Apparao Mallavarapu founder, ISRO/DRDO programs, defence electronics moat. Revenue borderline (Rs.953Cr consolidated).

### Step 3: Deep search for private precision machining companies

Searched Peenya Industrial Area specifically:
- "Top precision components manufacturers Bangalore Peenya"
- "Zenith Precision Bangalore aerospace CNC"
- "GI Auto Bengaluru IATF manufacturer"

**Key finds:**
- **Zenith Precision** — AS9100 + IATF certified, 3 units + aerospace-exclusive unit, Devanahalli Aerospace SEZ expansion in progress. Founded 1992 by Deepak Pinto. Private, revenue unknown.
- **GI Auto Pvt Ltd** — IATF certified, 4 units + warehouse, 50 years old, but near-zero public data for scoring.

### Step 4: Verification of each company

For every company, verified:
1. **E1 (Producer):** Company website product/services pages, IndiaMart listings, facility descriptions
2. **Revenue band:** BSE/NSE screener.in, ICRA ratings reports, Tofler, MCA filings, funding press releases
3. **Ownership structure:** MCA filings, Tracxn, BSE shareholding disclosures, Preqin
4. **Decision-maker:** Company About Us / Leadership pages, LinkedIn, alumni databases
5. **Growth signals:** Press releases, BSE announcements, IPO filings, LinkedIn hiring, website recency
6. **Systems maturity:** Certification lists (AS9100, IATF, NADCAP), company size, listed status, professional management

## Key Learnings About the Segment

1. **Revenue scaling is fast in aerospace:** Companies like Sansera, Dynamatic, and Suprajit are genuine Federer companies that grew past the ICP. Research must use *current* revenue, not historical filings.

2. **PSU presence is dominant:** BEL and HAL are the anchors of Bengaluru aerospace. Every search returns them — filter early.

3. **Private MSME data is thin:** Companies like GI Auto, Zenith, and similar Peenya manufacturers have minimal public data. Field research (direct visit or cold call) is needed to score C4, C7, C8 for these companies.

4. **NADCAP and AS9100D are strong C3 signals:** In this segment, these certifications are the clearest evidence of genuine differentiation. Companies with NADCAP approval (JJG, Centum, Maini) are credibly differentiated.

5. **Acquisition activity is high:** Maini Precision (acquired by Raymond) is an example of ICP companies being absorbed by larger groups. Always check recent MCA filings for stake changes.
